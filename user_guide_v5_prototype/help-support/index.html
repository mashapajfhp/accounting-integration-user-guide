🔌 Connection Issue Resolution

Example of successful connection status
Step 1: Check Connection Status
  • Go to Settings → Payroll → Accounting Integration
  • Look for green "Connected" status indicator
  • If red or disconnected, proceed to Step 2
Step 2: Reconnect Authentication
  • Click "Reconnect" or "Refresh Connection"
  • Complete OAuth flow in your accounting system
  • Verify admin permissions in your accounting software
Step 3: Platform-Specific Checks
  • Xero: Check app connection limits
  • QuickBooks: Verify company selection
  • NetSuite: Confirm token permissions
  • Dynamics 365: Check environment access
⚠️
Still having issues? Check your accounting system's status page for outages.

🔄 Sync Problem Resolution

Step 1: Verify Prerequisites
  • Ensure payroll is completed and approved
  • Check that all payroll items are mapped
  • Verify connection is active (green status)
Step 2: Check Error Messages
  • Review sync history for specific errors
  • Common issues: Rate limits, validation errors, timeouts
  • Note exact error codes for support
Step 3: Retry Strategies
  • Wait 5-10 minutes and retry sync
  • Try syncing smaller date ranges
  • Use preview mode to test before full sync
💡
Tip: Schedule syncs during off-peak hours for better performance.

📊 Data Issue Resolution

Step 1: Verify Mappings
  • Check payroll item to account mappings
  • Ensure cost centers are correctly assigned
  • Verify currency settings match
Step 2: Balance Validation
  • Confirm debits equal credits (QuickBooks requirement)
  • Check for rounding differences
  • Verify payment method mappings
Step 3: Data Reconciliation
  • Compare payroll totals with journal entries
  • Check for missing or duplicate transactions
  • Review cost center allocations
🔍
Use the preview function to verify data before syncing to catch issues early.

⚡ Performance Issue Resolution

Step 1: Identify Bottlenecks
  • Check payroll size (large employee count)
  • Review network connectivity
  • Verify accounting system performance
Step 2: API Rate Limits
  • QuickBooks: 500 requests/minute limit
  • Xero: 60 requests/minute, 5000/day limit
  • NetSuite: 1000 requests/hour limit
  • Wait if rate limited, then retry
Step 3: Optimization
  • Sync during off-peak hours
  • Consider batch processing for large payrolls
  • Break large syncs into smaller periods
📈
Performance typically improves during nights and weekends when API usage is lower.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I sync payroll data? ▼
Most organizations sync monthly after payroll completion. However, you can sync as often as needed. For accuracy, sync after finalizing each payroll period and before making any corrections.
What happens if my accounting software is down during sync? ▼
The sync will fail with a connection error. Your payroll data remains safe in Bayzat. Simply retry the sync once your accounting software is back online. No data is lost during failed syncs.
Can I sync historical payroll data? ▼
Yes, you can sync historical payroll periods. Select the specific period in the sync interface. However, be mindful of your accounting system's data retention policies and period closing rules.
Why are my journal entries unbalanced in QuickBooks? ▼
QuickBooks requires exact balance between debits and credits. Check your payroll item mappings, ensure all items are mapped to accounts, and verify that payment methods are correctly configured.
How do I handle multi-dimensional cost centers? ▼
Currently, multi-dimensional cost centers (e.g., Project + Department + Location) have limited support. Contact support for complex allocation requirements or consider using your accounting system's native tools for advanced cost allocation.
What should I do if sync takes longer than expected? ▼
Large payrolls or high API usage can slow syncs. Wait for completion (can take up to 30 minutes for large datasets). If it times out, retry during off-peak hours or contact support for optimization advice.
